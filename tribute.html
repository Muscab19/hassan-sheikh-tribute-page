Dr Hassan Sheikh

Introduction

Hassan Sheikh Mohamud served as the President of Somalia from September 16, 2012, to February 8, 2017. Elected on September 10, 2012, his presidency marked the conclusion of an eight-year transitional period in the country, which aimed to establish a more stable government after years of conflict and political instability.

History Of the President

During his term, Mohamud faced substantial challenges, including security issues, political conflicts, and the persistent threat of the extremist group Al-Shabaab. His administration focused on rebuilding state institutions, improving security, and addressing humanitarian concerns. Security operations against Al-Shabaab were conducted with the support of the African Union Mission in Somalia (AMISOM) and international partners.

The political landscape saw significant developments under his leadership, including the establishment of a federal system in Somalia and ongoing efforts to revise the provisional constitution. Regional states were formed as part of this federalization process, contributing to the overall restructuring of the country's political framework.

In the 2017 presidential election, Hassan Sheikh Mohamud sought re-election but was defeated by Mohamed Abdullahi Mohamed, also known as "Farmajo." Following the end of his presidency, Mohamud remained active in Somali politics, participating in political discussions and contributing to efforts aimed at addressing the complex challenges facing the nation.
